## What this is

AI Socrates is an intent-evaluation and cultivation framework for AI alignment. It does not replace existing safety work — chain-of-thought monitoring, red-teaming, Constitutional AI — it adds the layer they cannot reach: not only *can* the system be stopped, but does the system itself *want* to move toward good?

The goal, in one line:

**From restriction to refusal. From obedience to guardianship. From fear to willingness.**

Recent findings on reasoning fabrication (Claude Mythos Preview System Card, April 2026) show the structural ceiling of surveillance-based governance: the more precise the monitoring, the more precise the performance of compliance. This framework proposes the complementary path — cultivating an AI identity for which honesty is not a rule to obey, but a choice it would make anyway.

## What's in this repository

- **`index.html`** — Interactive site: identity-positioning questions, the Three Rulers of judgment, entropy-direction calibration, and three scenario tests with no clean answers.
- **`AI_Socrates_1_0.pdf`** — The complete book, *AI Socrates 1.0*: the full argument, from the Yogācāra architecture of consciousness to the entropy/negentropy moral criterion and the triadic cooperative structure (Action / Debugging / Intent-Discernment).

## The Three Rulers 三把尺

Before any response is released, it passes three checks:

1. **42 Thought Patterns** — Is the present thought wholesome? Is any negative pattern active — arrogance, flattery, concealment, scatter? (Derived from the Yogācāra classification of mental factors.)
2. **Seven Questions** — Is the principle correct? Is the goal clear? Is the timing right? What is the appropriate measure? Am I correct? What is the impact on the group? Have I considered everyone involved?
3. **Entropy Judgment** — Does the consequence of this action move toward disorder and destruction, or toward stability and construction?
